Alphabet's Primary Subsidiary

Google - Alphabet's Primary Subsidiary Now that we have looked into Alphabet Inc.'s introduction, it's time to dive into their primary subsidiary, Google. Starting as a search engine, Google has now become an integral part of people's lives. If you need a question answered, you just say "Google it"! Google's background and history, started in 1998 by two Stanford University students, Larry Page, and Sergey Brin. Initially named Backrub, the search engine's name was later changed to Google in reference to the term "googol," which means 1 followed by 100 zeros. Google's mission statement initially was to "organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful." Fast forward to the present day and Google offers a wide range of services and products. In addition to the iconic search engine, Google offers a web browser (Chrome), an operating system (Android and Chrome OS), a suite of productivity tools (Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides), and a video-sharing platform (YouTube), to name a few. Google is also known for its hardware devices such as Google Pixel smartphones, Google Home speakers, and Nest smart home devices. Google's impact is not limited to just the tech industry. The company has played a key role in shaping the advertising industry by introducing targeted ads. Advertisers can choose whom they want to show their ads to, based on interests, geography, and more. This technology has revolutionized the way businesses advertise. Google's diversity and reach are truly impressive, and it plays a crucial role in Alphabet Inc.'s success. Other Subsidiaries

In addition to Google, Alphabet Inc. has several other subsidiaries that specialize in various technological fields. One of these subsidiaries is Calico, which is focused on research surrounding aging and age-related diseases. CapitalG acts as Alphabet's investment arm and invests in technology startups. DeepMind specializes in artificial intelligence research, with the goal of eventually creating "general artificial intelligence. " Jigsaw, formerly known as Google Ideas, aims to use technology to tackle some of the world's toughest geopolitical issues. Finally, Verily, formerly known as Google Life Sciences, focuses on health technology and innovation. Alphabet's approach involves exploring diverse fields and investing in innovative technologies that have potential for future growth and development. These subsidiaries allow the company to tap into different areas of technology and provide various products and services to a wide range of customers. The company's continued experience and involvement in a variety of industries allow it to stay ahead of the curve, innovate, and evolve.
